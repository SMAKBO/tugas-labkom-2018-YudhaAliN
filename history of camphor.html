The History of Camphor

Barus Town is a sub-district in Tapanuli Tengah District, North Sumatra, Indonesia. From this city the name "Kapur Barus", or people more familiar with camphor, originated and famous since time immemorial.

Camphor are made from wood chopped pieces of Cinnamomum Camphora tree that grows in the area of ​​Barus. These small pieces of wood are boiled and through the process of distillation and crystallization obtained by camphor crystals as raw materials for processing in the factory.

So famous is the town of Barus as a producer of camphor raw materials since the 9th century, until all merchants from around the world sailed to Barus to buy this camper wood.

This camphor by the public is used as fragrances of clothes, rengat repellent, and non-food preservatives. Milk camphor has always been an important trading commodity. The term lime is used by traders from India, Southeast Asia and the Middle East for liquids drained by camphor tree extraction (Cinnamomum Camphora).
